# Cyber Security Task 01: Personal Security Audit & Cyber Awareness Assessment

## Objective
The purpose of this task is to assess personal digital security practices, identify potential risks, and build foundational cybersecurity awareness — starting with securing yourself before securing systems and networks.

## Folder Contents

### `01_Device_Security_Assessment.md`
A security check of my personal laptop covering OS edition and version (Windows 11 Pro, 25H2), update status, antivirus (Microsoft Defender — active, clean scan), and firewall status across all network profiles. Supported by 4 screenshots in the `screenshots/` subfolder.

### `02_Password_Policy.md`
Answers to key questions about password security — what makes a password strong, why uniqueness matters, what MFA is, and the risks of password reuse — followed by a personal 8-point password policy.

### `03_Account_Security_Review.md`
A review of 3 real accounts (Google, GitHub, Instagram) checking whether MFA is enabled, whether the password is strong, and whether a recovery email is set — presented in a summary table with no actual credentials included.

### `04_Threat_Research.md`
Research on 5 major cyber threats — Phishing, Malware, Ransomware, Social Engineering, and Data Breach — each covered with a definition, a real-world example, and prevention methods.

### `05_Awareness_Poster_StrongPasswords.png`
A designed awareness poster on the topic of Strong Password Security, covering what to avoid and best practices, built around the concept "Your Password Is the Door."

### `06_Reflection_Report.md`
A 300+ word personal reflection on what this audit revealed, what habits will change as a result, and why cybersecurity matters in today's world.

### `screenshots/`
Supporting screenshots for Part A: `os_version.jpg`, `update_status.jpg`, `antivirus.jpg`, `firewall.jpg`.

## Key Takeaways
- Verified device security settings across OS, antivirus, and firewall
- Built a personal password policy based on current best practices
- Reviewed MFA, password strength, and recovery settings across 3 accounts
- Researched the five most common categories of cyber threats with real-world context
- Designed a strong passwords awareness poster
- Reflected on security gaps and improvements to maintain going forward
