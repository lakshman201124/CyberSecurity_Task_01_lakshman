# Cyber Security Task 02: CIA Triad & Risk Assessment

## Objective
This task focuses on understanding the foundational principles of cybersecurity through the CIA Triad, analysing real-world attacks, and developing practical risk assessment and security recommendation skills for an institutional environment.


## Folder Contents

### `01_CIA_Triad_Research.md`
In-depth explanation of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability — each with a definition, explanation of importance, and a real-world example (Bangladesh Bank heist for Integrity, Mirai botnet DDoS for Availability). Includes a comparison table.

### `02_Cyber_Attack_Case_Study.md`
A case study on the 2020 SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack — covering what happened, which CIA Triad principles were violated, the scale of impact across US government agencies and major corporations, and how it could have been prevented.

### `03_Risk_Assessment_Table.md`
A risk assessment for a college environment identifying 8 digital assets (including student portal, email system, payroll, cloud storage, and campus Wi-Fi), their associated threats, impact severity, and risk levels — with reasoning for each rating.

### `04_Threat_Analysis.md`
Detailed research on 5 threat categories — Insider Threats, Malware, Phishing, Weak Passwords, and Unpatched Systems — each with a description, possible impact, and practical prevention methods.

### `05_Security_Recommendations.md`
10 prioritized security recommendations for the college, each with an explanation of why it matters — covering MFA, patch management, email security controls, user training, RBAC, backups, cloud storage governance, incident response, network segmentation, and security audits.

### `06_Consultant_Report.md`
A professional one-page consultant report answering "How can we improve our cybersecurity posture?" — identifying the top 5 risks, top 5 recommendations, and expected benefits of implementation.

## Key Takeaways
- Understood the CIA Triad as the lens through which every security decision should be evaluated
- Analysed the SolarWinds supply chain attack and its implications for software trust
- Practiced risk assessment across a realistic institutional environment with 8 assets
- Researched 5 major threat categories with real-world context and actionable prevention
- Developed 10 prioritized security recommendations grounded in the risk assessment
- Produced a professional consultant report suitable for an institutional audience
